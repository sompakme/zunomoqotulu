    A look into Tyson Fury's 550k Morecambe Mansion Ahead of Heavyweight Doubleheader Against Anthony

    - Tyson Fury and Anthony Joshua have agreed to face-off in a doubleheader fight - The winner will go home with all four belts after the two-legged affair at an undisclosed date - Ahead of the encounter, the 32-year-old has made his home a comfortable place after training

    Tyson Fury will be counting on the tactics that helped him overcome Deontay Wilder when he faces Anthony Joshua at a yet-to-be-disclosed date and venue.

    The 32-year-old handed the Bronze Bomber his first career defeat and claim the WBC title over a year ago in las Vegas.

    And he has already shifted focus to fighting his fellow Briton in a unified heavyweight bout with the winner going away with all the belts.

    While his next fight has forced him to put in a little more hard work, the Gypsy King is equally serious about making his Morecambe home a lot more peaceful and relaxing.

    People often flock to watch Fury train on the beach with the Gypsy King, wife Paris, and five children living in a £550,000 home.

    "People say to me 'why don't you live in California or America?' - why would I?”

    Fury previously said.

    “Why would I abandon my own country for a bit of money and some fame? I love it. I would never leave Morecambe. It's true beauty. If the weather was better, every house here would be a million quid."

    Daily Star reveals that the WBC champion recently took delivery of three large-screen TVs, which also features a nice kitchen and dining area.

    An ITV documentary showed the Fury family living at home with dedicated areas to the success that Tyson has had in the ring.

    A large L-shaped sofa is big enough for the whole family to relax on at the end of the day while the entrance hall has white glossy tiles for flooring, and baroque print cream and gold wallpaper.

    There’s space outside for when the sun shines with Paris sharing a photo on social media of herself and Tyson enjoying a brown wicker dining set.

    Fury is estimated to be worth around £100m, and he will be earning plenty more from the fight with Joshua. And he does have luxuries at home too, including having a Ferrari and Rolls Royce parked up.

    Legit.ng earlier reported that Boxing promoter Bob Arum has disclosed that the date for the undisputed bout between Anthony Joshua and Tyson Fury will be made public within two weeks.

    The highly-anticipated showdown will soon be finalised according to the promoter as talks over the ‘Battle of Britain’ resumed in December.

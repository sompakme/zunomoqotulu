    Central Standard Time vs. Daylight Saving Time: The Great Illinois Time Tango

    Illinois operates on a two-step system, folks. There's Central Standard Time (CST), which is 5 hours behind Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) – the fancy way of saying "world time." But then, whammo! We switch to Daylight Saving Time (DST) in the spring, which throws things forward an hour. So, Illinois ends up being Central Daylight Time (CDT) during those brighter months. ☀️ That means Illinois is currently one hour ahead of standard time.

    But wait, there's more! Since today is May 30th, 2024, we're smack dab in the middle of DST. Illinois is currently on Central Daylight Time (CDT).

    So, what time is it in Illinois right now? It's 10:15 PM CDT!

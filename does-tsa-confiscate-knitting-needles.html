    Yes, TSA (Transportation Security Administration) does confiscate knitting needles at airport security checkpoints. Knitting needles are considered sharp objects and fall under the list of prohibited items that cannot be carried onto an airplane. This rule is in place to ensure the safety of all passengers and to prevent any potential threats or harm during the flight.

    Knitting needles, along with other sharp objects like scissors, metal nail files, and razor blades, are not allowed in carry-on bags. However, if you still wish to travel with knitting needles, you can pack them in your checked baggage. It is important to note that different airlines and security regulations may vary, so it is recommended to check with your specific airline carrier or the TSA website for the most up-to-date information regarding prohibited items.

    FAQs about TSA confiscation of knitting needles:

    1. Can I bring crochet hooks on a plane?

    Yes, crochet hooks are generally allowed on a plane as they are not considered as sharp as knitting needles. However, it is always best to check with your specific airline or the TSA website for any restrictions or guidelines.

    2. Can I bring circular knitting needles on a plane?

    Circular knitting needles are usually allowed on a plane. They are considered safer compared to straight knitting needles as they have a flexible cable between the needles, minimizing the risk of accidental injury during a flight. However, it is advisable to contact your airline or check the TSA guidelines for any specific requirements.

    3. What about wooden knitting needles?

    Wooden knitting needles are generally permitted in carry-on bags. These tend to be less sharp and therefore pose a lower risk than metal knitting needles. However, it is always recommended to verify the guidelines of your airline or the TSA to ensure compliance.

It is always recommended to check the TSA guidelines or contact your airline before packing your supplies.

    6. Can I bring knitting needles in my carry-on if I have a medical condition?

    If you have a medical condition that requires you to carry knitting needles or any other prohibited item, it is advisable to contact your airline in advance. Some airlines may allow exceptions or require additional documentation to authorize the transport of medical needles or equipment.

    If you accidentally bring knitting needles in your carry-on baggage, it is likely that they will be detected during the security screening process.     11. Can I bring knitting needles on a domestic flight?

    Even for domestic flights, TSA regulations apply at security checkpoints in U.S. airports. Therefore, knitting needles are generally not allowed in carry-on bags. However, you can pack them in your checked baggage for domestic flights. It is still advisable to check with your specific airline or refer to the TSA guidelines for any further details or potential updates.

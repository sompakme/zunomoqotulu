    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Carol Kaye was born on March 24, 1935, in Everett, Washington. She began her music career as a guitar player in the 1950s, but it was her switch to bass guitar in the 1960s that catapulted her to fame. She became a part of the legendary group of studio musicians known as The Wrecking Crew, contributing to some of the biggest hits of the era.

    Peak of Her Career

    During the 1960s and 1970s, Kaye was one of the most in-demand session musicians in the industry. She worked with renowned artists like Frank Sinatra, The Beach Boys, and Simon & Garfunkel, among others. Her contributions to these iconic songs significantly boosted her earnings and reputation in the industry.

    Retirement and Current Activities

    Real Estate Investments

    Margot Robbie won’t stop dressing like a Barbie doll—and we’re not mad about it.

    The actor has once again tapped into the Mattel doll’s sartorial archives to re-create another glamorous Barbie look. At a press conference in Seoul earlier this week, Robbie wore a hot-pink Moschino skirt suit consisting of a fitted lapel-less blazer, an asymmetrical midriff-baring crop top, and a miniskirt. The trio of pieces—along with a matching hot-pink pillbox hat and a heart-shaped purse with a quilted exterior—were all bedazzled with glittering crystal studs. Finally, she topped the look off with bright pink heeled mules from Manolo Blahnik and pearl earrings from Tiffany & Co.

    Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

    Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

    Celeb stylist Andrew Mukamal shared a picture of the outfit’s inspiration on Instagram: a 1964-era “Sparkling Pink” Barbie dressed in a shiny baby-pink evening coat, draped midi dress, and bow-adorned hat.

    Han Myung-Gu//Getty Images

    Mukamal is the visionary behind Robbie’s rotation of Barbie-inspired outfits during the rollout for Greta Gerwig’s highly anticipated Barbie movie, which officially drops in theaters later this month. Together, the stylist and actor have created a memorable press-tour wardrobe that often references the iconic doll’s storied fashion history.

    For instance, at an appearance in Australia last month, Mukamal and Robbie drew inspiration from the black-and-white swimsuit worn by the original 1959 Barbie. Instead of wearing an exact replica of the one-piece, Robbie stunned in a black-and-white striped minidress by Hervé Léger, along with a pair of glossy black heels from Manolo Blahnik.

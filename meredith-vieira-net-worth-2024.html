    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Meredith Vieira was born on December 30, 1953, in Providence, Rhode Island. She graduated from Tufts University with a degree in English in 1975. Vieira began her career in journalism as a news announcer for WORC radio in Worcester, Massachusetts, before moving on to work as a reporter and anchor at WJAR-TV in Providence.

    Breakthrough in Television

    Vieira’s breakthrough came in 1985 when she joined CBS News as a reporter. She later became a co-anchor on the CBS Morning News and contributed to “60 Minutes.” Her work on CBS significantly boosted her career and net worth.

    Joining “The View”

    In 1997, Vieira became one of the original panelists of ABC’s daytime talk show “The View,” where she stayed for nine years. Her stint on “The View” not only increased her visibility but also added to her growing net worth.

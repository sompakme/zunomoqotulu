    Guava leaves health benefits and side effects

    Psidium Guajava Linnaeus or guava is a source of nutrients for health. People consume both juices of guava and tea made of leaves. People, who live abroad, can also use this remedial drink. It is usually sold in the form of pre-bagged tea in the natural health grocers and stores.

    To make a medicinal beverage is rather simple:

    • Prepare a pot and heat some water in it.
    • Take a teapot, put some green leaves or pre-bagged tea there. Leave to brew. Green leaves should be steep for 15 minutes, while tea bags require only 4-5 minutes.
    • Strain the mixture. Enjoy it with lemon or honey!

    4 guava leaf tea side effects

    As plants with medicinal properties, guava trees also have limitations. There are only a few ones, but they are essential:

  • Drinking too much guava leaf tea leads to constipation.
  • It is forbidden for pregnant women as may cause complications.
  • If you take any other medicine (especially if it is pressure or diabetes medicines), you should avoid adding guava leaves to tea.
  • The microelements of guava tree may cause allergies. However, it happens rather seldom.

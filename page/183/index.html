Why is there a ball in Guinness cans?

01 Aug 2024, 00:00

INSIDE your Guinness can lurks a secret - a tiny plastic ball that makes sure the iconic creamy head is achieved. Called widgets, these plastic balls blast the stout beer with nitrogen gas to aerate the head, making your Guinness taste as though it was just poured fresh from the tap. Why is there a ball in Guinness cans? Nitrogenisation is the key to making a beer that’s rich and creamy, with a smooth texture.
